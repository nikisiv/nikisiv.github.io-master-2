MakeGlow

MakeGlow is a fully functional solar lamp made of discarded cardboard. It is easy to assemble and is low-cost. It is mainly intended to be built by and for underprivileged students in developing nations.

Problem

India is facing a severe case of energy poverty. More than one-fourth of population comprising the rural poor (292 million) lacks access to electricity and uses kerosene lamps for lighting purposes. It is well known that solar lanterns are a better alternative. In spite of entering the current market, the solar lanterns have not been successful in fulfilling peopleâ€™s light-related needs. The main reasons for this are high cost and lack of awareness among the rural poor population. Make Glow is a unique approach to address both of the above-mentioned issues directly.

The Product

Single-piece template and simple geometry composed mainly of straight lines that facilitates easy cuts and folds.
